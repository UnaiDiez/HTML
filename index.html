Tesis Doctoral

Título: Simulación Realista del Tráfico de una Red de Ferrocarril

Objetivos Principales:

  1. Desarrollar un modelo de simulación que represente de manera precisa el tráfico ferroviario.
  2. Analizar la eficiencia operativa y la seguridad de la red de ferrocarril.
  3. Proponer mejoras basadas en los resultados de la simulación para optimizar el flujo de tráfico y reducir retrasos.

Metodología:

El estudio utiliza técnicas avanzadas de modelado y simulación, incorporando datos reales de tráfico y operativos de redes ferroviarias. Se implementan algoritmos de simulación que permiten evaluar diferentes escenarios y estrategias de gestión del tráfico.

Resultados y Conclusiones:

A través de la simulación realista, se identificaron varios cuellos de botella y oportunidades de mejora en la red ferroviaria. Las recomendaciones derivadas del estudio proponen cambios en la gestión operativa y en la infraestructura que podrían mejorar significativamente la eficiencia y la seguridad del sistema ferroviario.

The efficient management of real-time network traffic is crucial in the railway sector, being a significant resource consumer. The objective is to enhance network capacity, reliability, safety, and reduce energy expenditure by replacing manual rescheduling with automatic planning algorithms and transitioning to a flexible traffic control model [1].

Railway operations often face disturbances causing train delays, either being minor ones, affecting a single train or set of trains, with normalcy restored easily, or more complex delays spreading across the network [2]. Two disturbance management approaches exist: contingency plans with pre-planned recovery strategies (limited in covering all scenarios), and mathematical recovery models (more efficient for rescheduling) [3]. Disturbances are categorized as smaller disruptions (modifiable by adjusting train schedules) whereas disruptions are considered as major incidents, requiring changes in schedules, resources, and personnel, with the research focusing on the latter [4].

The main objective of this research is to establish optimal traffic recovery strategies for a railway network in the face of disruptions, using the rescheduling of necessary resources through automatic algorithms in a decision support tool.

This tool will include the rescheduling of services, as well as the management of rolling stock and necessary personnel. A simulator that uses this tool will be also developed to check the results of the solutions and gather data from the simulations to improve the models.

To achieve this, the following resources are available:

  • OPTICON simulation tool, developed at CEIT
  • European project context in which the thesis is framed (FP1-MOTIONAL)
  • Real operational data (presumably data from L3 Metro Bilbao)

It is proposed to advance in the study of solutions through mathematical algorithms for disruptions that affect schedules, rolling stock and personnel. Then, a simplified version of the software will be developed, which only will receive an alarm trigger of a problem and depending on the case, different solutions will be defined by the algorithm. A network model will be used in a controlled environment to simulate small disturbances and analyze the response obtained from the initial simple algorithm for disturbances. Larger disturbances will then be introduced to evaluate replanning. These results will serve as a reference for future measurements and comparisons, as well as help in the development of a more complete algorithm for disruptions.
In the figures (c) and (d), possible solutions due to a delay of a service are shown. In the first case, the main objective is to reduce the delay of the firstly delayed service as much as possible. Following that objective, other services are affected due to the restrictions of available routes in the network, so they suffer consecutive delays. On the other hand, in the second case, delaying even more the already delayed service the planned traffic operations can be maintained with only one service affected, depending on which is the most important aspect for the control center.
